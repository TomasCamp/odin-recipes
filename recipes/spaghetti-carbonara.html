Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its creamy sauce and rich, savory flavor. This recipe uses simple ingredients to create a delicious and satisfying meal.

  • 12 ounces (340g) of spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup (240ml) of grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 4 ounces (115g) of pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous pinch of black pepper.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy and golden. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for a minute.
  4. Remove the skillet from the heat and add the cooked spaghetti. Toss the pasta in the pancetta and garlic mixture.
  5. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss everything together until the pasta is coated in the creamy sauce.
  6. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a bit of pasta cooking water to achieve the desired consistency.
  7. Serve the Spaghetti Carbonara immediately, garnished with chopped fresh parsley. Enjoy!
